Bribery and Solicitation of Bribery (Vote
 
Trading).
  
Article V, section 40 of the 
Colorado Constitution prohibits
 
bribery
, which is how the constitution refers to vote 
trading.  A legislator who actually gives his or her vote or influence in favor of or 
against a measure in exchange for an agreeme
nt by another legislator to give his or her 
vote or influence in favor of or against a different measure has committed 
bribery
.  An 
offer or promise to do the same made by both members

which objective is 
presumably not achieved

constitutes 
solicitation of 
bribery
.  Any member or person 
elected to the General Assembly who is found guilty of either offense must be 
expelled.  It is not a violation of this constitutional provision for one or more 
legislators to make an agreement with individuals who are not leg
is
l
ators (such as 
lobbyists) to support or oppose different 
measures.
